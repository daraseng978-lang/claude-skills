# Comedy Frameworks for Short-Form Reels

Reference for `funny-reels` skill. Pick ONE framework per reel — stacking kills the payoff.

## 1. Rule of 3

Two normal items establish the pattern, third breaks it.

**Example (POV, 18s):**
- "Things founders say on day 1: 'We're changing the world.'"
- "Things founders say on day 30: 'We pivoted.'"
- "Things founders say on day 90: 'Does anyone want to buy a slightly used domain name?'"

**Why it works:** Brain predicts third item, reality violates prediction → laugh.

## 2. Misdirection (Bait-and-switch)

Setup points to punchline A, delivery is punchline B.

**Example (12s):**
- VO: "The secret to running a 7-figure agency…"
- Beat (hold 2s)
- VO: "…is inheriting 7 figures."

**Why it works:** Violates expectation of a value-bomb reel with a cynical truth.

## 3. Escalation

Same joke, three sizes bigger.

**Example (20s):**
- "Me at 9 AM: Let's crush this sprint."
- "Me at 1 PM: We'll finish by end of day."
- "Me at 11 PM, eating cereal in the dark: The feature was the friends we made along the way."

**Why it works:** Commits to the bit. Each beat raises stakes — the absurdity compounds.

## 4. Callback (Running gag)

Reference an earlier reel. Rewards loyal viewers, gives new viewers curiosity.

**Example:** If a previous reel had "the CEO who can't open a PDF," drop him into an unrelated skit silently in the background.

**Why it works:** Followers feel in on the joke → shares + saves.

## 5. Contrast

Expectation vs reality. Past vs present. Boss vs intern. Stock photo vs real life.

**Example (15s):**
- Split screen: "Founders on LinkedIn" (glossy shots, coffee on rooftop) vs "Founders at 2 AM" (hoodie, energy drink, 47 tabs open).

**Why it works:** Universal recognition — viewer sees themselves.

## 6. Relatable-Specific

The weirdly specific detail that makes viewers go "how did you know??"

**Example (10s):**
- "The exact moment you realize you CC'd the wrong Amanda on the offer letter."
- Frozen face reaction, slow zoom, dramatic music.

**Why it works:** Specificity > generic. Generic relatable feels manufactured; specific feels stolen from viewer's brain.

## 7. Character Commitment

Pick a heightened persona and stay in it the whole reel. Don't break character for a punchline.

**Example:** "Corporate email translator" — types "Per my last email," VO delivers "Are you stupid?"

**Why it works:** Character-driven reels build series potential — audience comes back for the character, not the joke.

## 8. Observational Absurdism

Point out something mundane, then treat it like it's insane.

**Example:** "Why do we call it 'a standing meeting' when everyone just stands more awkwardly?"

**Why it works:** Low effort to shoot (talking head), high shareability.

## 9. Confident Narrator Power Fantasy

A single confident character monologues a delusional first-person "how I'm going to take over" story. Cuts to absurd AI-generated scenes illustrating each claim. Escalates scene by scene.

**Structure:**
- Narrator stays in one "hero" shot that recurs (e.g. at a desk, behind a podium)
- Every 5s, cut to an absurd cutaway that literalizes what they just said
- Claims get bigger: personal goal → industry domination → interplanetary
- Narrator never breaks confidence, even as scenes get ridiculous

**Example (90s, cartoon startup founder character):**
- "In my first week, I closed 3 deals." → cutaway: founder shaking hand with a confused alien
- "By month 3, we'll IPO." → cutaway: Toronto skyline with the founder's face on every billboard
- "And within a year, I replace Sam Altman." → cutaway: Silicon Valley executives fainting dramatically

**Why it works:** Commits HARD to the bit. The gap between stated confidence and visual absurdity is the joke. Perfect for AI-gen video — every claim gets its own surreal shot. See `references/style-inspo-rahrah.md` for production guide and safe-target concept list.

**Do not use this format for:** real named people, real political groups, or detention/violence imagery. Punch at archetypes and systems, not individuals.

## Combining (Advanced)

You CAN layer frameworks across a series, not within a single reel. Example:
- Reel 1: Character (corporate email translator) + Misdirection
- Reel 2: Same character + Rule of 3
- Reel 3: Same character + Callback to Reel 1

This is how characters become franchises.

## What to Cut

If you find yourself:
- Explaining the joke before it lands → cut the explanation
- Adding a second punchline "just in case" → cut the second one
- Using music to tell viewer it's funny → let the writing do the work
- Writing a joke that requires > 3 seconds of context → it's a tweet, not a reel
