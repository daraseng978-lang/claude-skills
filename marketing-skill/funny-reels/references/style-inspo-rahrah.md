# Style Inspo — AI Cartoon Monologue Reels (Rahrah House Style)

Reference for `funny-reels` skill. Captures the visual + structural style of high-polish AI 3D comedy reels popular on Facebook Reels, applied to Rahrah Media's safe-comedy subject list.

**What we're copying:** the craft — AI 3D render, karaoke captions, 5s scene cadence, confident-narrator structure.
**What we're NOT copying:** named politicians, real public figures, detention/arrest imagery, party-based targeting.

---

## Visual Style Spec

### Render
- **AI 3D cartoon / Pixar-esque**: rounded features, big expressive eyes, slightly exaggerated proportions
- **Tools**: Midjourney / ChatGPT image gen for stills, Runway / Kling / Sora / Hailuo for animated scenes
- **Consistency**: use the same character-prompt locked across scenes — define hair, outfit, age, vibe once and reuse

### Lighting & camera
- **Cinematic lighting** — warm key light, soft shadows
- **Shallow depth of field** (background slightly blurred)
- **Camera angles vary**: hero shot (front-center), over-shoulder, push-in close-up
- **Color palette**: saturated, high contrast

### Aspect & framing
- **1080 × 1920 (9:16)** vertical
- **Subject centered in middle third** — leave bottom 400px for captions, top 220px clear of platform UI

## Narrative Structure — "Confident Narrator Power Fantasy"

A single character delivers a delusional monologue. Every 5 seconds, cut to a surreal cutaway that literalizes the claim. Claims escalate.

### Beat map (60-90s reel, 12-18 scenes)

| Section | Duration | What's on screen |
|---------|----------|------------------|
| Intro | 0-5s | Narrator in hero pose — establishes character |
| Hook claim | 5-10s | "I'm going to [outrageous goal]" |
| Escalation 1 | 10-25s | 3 cutaways illustrating step 1 (small wins) |
| Escalation 2 | 25-45s | 3 cutaways illustrating step 2 (industry-level) |
| Escalation 3 | 45-70s | 3 cutaways illustrating step 3 (global / absurd) |
| Button | 70-90s | Return to hero shot, final punchline, loop frame |

### Pacing rules
- **One scene every 4-6 seconds.** No scene lingers.
- **Narrator VO is continuous** across all cutaways — the audio doesn't cut when the picture does
- **Recurring hero shot** — cut back to the narrator every 15-20s so the viewer stays anchored
- **Escalation must be visual, not just verbal** — each scene outdoes the last

## Caption Style (Karaoke)

- **Style**: bold chunky sans-serif (Poppins Black, Impact, or similar)
- **Two-tone**: active word in **yellow**, rest in **white**, all with **thick black stroke**
- **Placement**: lower third, centered, ~3-4 words on screen at a time
- **Timing**: sync to spoken word — each word highlights as it's said
- **Never cover the face** of the speaker

### Tool recipe
- CapCut, Submagic, or Opus Clip → "karaoke" or "highlight" preset
- Or Premiere Pro + Essential Graphics template with keyframed color

---

## Voiceover

- **AI voice** (ElevenLabs, OpenAI TTS, Suno) — confident, slightly theatrical delivery
- **Slight reverb / compression** to feel "speech-like" not "narrator-like"
- **Pacing**: ~150-170 words/minute — faster than default TTS
- **Character consistency**: lock one voice across a series so the character becomes recognizable

## Safe Subject List

**Punch at archetypes, systems, and roles — NOT identifiable individuals or protected groups.**

### ✅ SAFE targets
- Delusional startup founder / serial pivoter
- Overconfident intern or new hire
- Burnt-out middle manager
- Tech industry archetypes: crypto bro, AI evangelist, VC, "thought leader"
- Corporate roles: HR enforcer, IT gatekeeper, sales hype-man
- Suburban tropes: grill dad, yoga mom, HOA president, minivan driver
- Overly-invested hobbyists: sneakerheads, wine guys, gym influencer
- Original mascots / fictional heroes: "The AI That Got Tired," "CEO Cat," "The Stapler That Had Enough"
- Industry satire: consultant speak, LinkedIn influencer, podcast bro

### ❌ OFF-LIMITS
- Named real politicians — Trump, Biden, Harris, Obama, any elected official
- Named public figures / celebrities / CEOs (even as caricature — right-of-publicity risk)
- Real political parties or movements (Democrats, Republicans, MAGA, etc.)
- Detention, arrest, deportation, raid imagery
- Militarized law enforcement (ICE, police, army) acting against any group
- Race, ethnicity, religion, gender identity, sexual orientation as punchline target
- Real brands being attacked by name (parody is legal but risky — clear with legal first)
- Anything referencing real mass-casualty events

If a concept requires a real person or political group to land, **reject it and rework**.

## Production Workflow

1. **Pick concept** from safe list (or brief a new one — run it past the OFF-LIMITS list)
2. **Lock the character** — one image-gen prompt, refined to consistency. Save it.
3. **Write VO script** — 150-170 wpm, ~180-230 words for a 75s reel
4. **Storyboard** — one line per 5s scene, matching VO to visual claim
5. **Generate assets** — image/video gen tool of choice, keep character locked
6. **Assemble** in CapCut / Premiere — cuts on beat, karaoke captions synced
7. **Audit against brand-safety checklist** in SKILL.md
8. **Export per platform** (see `platform-specs.md`)

## When to Use This Style

- **Use** when topic suits a confident-narrator monologue and you have time/budget for 12-18 AI-gen scenes
- **Don't use** for quick reactive reels, trend remixes, or talking-head POVs — it's high-effort and needs the full structure to land
- **Don't use** without the character-consistency lock — inconsistent character across scenes kills the bit
