# Pricing and Packaging

Pricing is the single highest-leverage lever in a productized service. Get it wrong and no amount of agent efficiency compensates.

## Three-Tier Rule

Offer exactly three tiers. Research (Van Westendorp + MaxDiff on small samples) consistently shows three tiers maximizes revenue versus one, two, or four.

- **Starter** — smallest unit that delivers standalone value. Attracts price-sensitive accounts and gives the Acquire agent a low-friction entry offer.
- **Growth** — the "designed winner." 60–70% of customers should end up here. Price and features are tuned so it's the obvious best value.
- **Scale** — 2–3x the Growth price with meaningfully more volume and higher-touch add-ons. Anchors the comparison and lifts ARPU.

Default for the SEO content business:

| Tier | Price | Volume | Used as |
|---|---|---|---|
| Starter | $2,000/mo | 10 articles | Entry + price anchor floor |
| Growth | $4,000/mo | 20 articles | Designed winner |
| Scale | $8,000/mo | 40 articles | High-end anchor + expansion target |

## The Anchoring Math

The Scale tier exists mostly to make Growth feel like the smart choice. If customers routinely pick Scale, you priced the tiers too close — widen the spread.

## Annual Pre-Pay

Offer 15% off for 12-month pre-pay. Benefits:

- **Cash flow:** 10.2 months of revenue upfront.
- **Churn optics:** Annual customers technically can't churn mid-term (they can refuse renewal, but it's a different risk profile).
- **Selection effect:** Buyers who pre-pay tend to be higher-intent and lower-maintenance.

Do not exceed 15%. Beyond that, you're leaking margin to solve a cash problem that probably doesn't exist.

## Overage Policy

Customers request more than their tier. Default policy:

- Allow up to +25% overage at **$180/article** (Starter/Growth) or **$150/article** (Scale).
- Beyond +25%, require tier upgrade.
- The Deliver agent never commits to overage without Head agent approval.

## Pricing Experiments — Allowed / Forbidden

**Allowed:**
- Raising prices for **new** customers. Increment 10% every 12 months. Grandfather existing.
- Tier renaming. Underlying prices stay.
- Bundling (e.g., "3 months Growth + 1 month free" as a Q4 promo, once).

**Forbidden without founder approval:**
- Discounts (except annual pre-pay).
- Custom pricing below tier floor.
- "Limited time" tactics that damage long-term price integrity.
- Free trials for Starter. (A single free sample article is OK; a 30-day trial is not.)

## Why No Discounts

Discounts:
- Attract price-sensitive customers who churn faster.
- Erode the Scale-to-Growth anchor.
- Train the agent that prices are negotiable, which compounds badly.

The discipline here is the paperclip principle: MRR, not logo count.

## Custom Enterprise Tier

Allowed only at **$15,000/mo+** with:
- 12-month commit.
- Founder on the signature.
- Dedicated onboarding call.
- Clear contract-defined SLA.

Below $15k/mo, say "we only offer standard tiers." The answer is in the product page. If they push, they're not your ICP.

## Price Increase Playbook

Every 12 months:
1. Head agent flags the anniversary.
2. Founder reviews and signs off.
3. Acquire agent updates the pricing page.
4. Existing customers grandfathered for 12 additional months, then migrate.
5. Communicate to existing: "Your price is locked through $DATE. New customers starting $DATE pay $NEW_PRICE."

Expect < 2% churn attributable to the increase. Net revenue goes up meaningfully.

## Payment Mechanics

- Stripe only. Card and ACH (for US Scale-tier customers).
- Invoice + Net-30 available on Scale only, with signed MSA.
- Auto-renew on. Cancel requires email to support@; the Head agent processes within one business day and logs every churn reason.

## Signals That Pricing Is Wrong

- **Close rate > 40%** → underpriced. Raise 20% and watch.
- **Close rate < 10%** → overpriced OR wrong ICP. Diagnose before cutting price.
- **> 20% of customers on Starter** → Growth tier isn't differentiated enough. Widen the gap.
- **> 30% requests for custom pricing** → packaging unclear. Re-audit the offer page.
