# 03 — Niche Selection

## The three axes

A profitable KDP niche lives at the intersection of:

1. **Proven demand** — top 10 listings have BSR < 100,000.
2. **Beatable competition** — review median < 500, big-publisher share < 30%.
3. **Sane price point** — top 10 median is $7.99–$10.99 paperback.

Miss one axis and you ship into a graveyard.

## Where to look for niches

- **Amazon autocomplete.** Type "word search" into the Amazon search bar — the dropdown shows the highest-velocity long-tails.
- **Amazon's "Customers also bought" carousel.** Click a winning SKU and the carousel reveals adjacent niches.
- **Amazon Best Sellers pages.** `amazon.com/gp/bestsellers/books/` drilled into Puzzles & Games > Word Search, Sudoku, Mazes, Cryptograms.
- **Google Trends.** Type "sudoku book" vs "word search book" to compare seasonality.
- **Gift-giving occasions.** Mother's Day, Father's Day, Christmas — search intent spikes 4–8 weeks before.

## The evergreen 6 (start here)

| Niche | Daily searches | Competition | Why it works |
|---|---|---|---|
| Large-print word search for seniors | High | Medium | Aging demographic + gift market |
| Sudoku (easy, medium, hard per book) | High | High | Global recognition, easy to segment |
| Mazes for kids 4–6 / 6–8 / 8–12 | High | Medium | Parent spend, summer/school cycles |
| Themed word search (gardening, cats, travel) | Medium | Low | Long-tail gift-buyer intent |
| Cryptograms / logic puzzles | Medium | Low | Small but loyal audience |
| Crossword for beginners | Medium | Medium | Underserved vs expert crosswords |

## What to avoid

- **Adult coloring books.** Saturated beyond belief; you need a brand to rank.
- **Anything with a celebrity, brand, or franchise name.** Copyright/trademark risk is existential.
- **Journals and notebooks with lines only.** 99% commodity; ranking requires paid ads.
- **Activity books bundling 20 different puzzle types.** Dilutes search signal. A book is either a sudoku book or it isn't.
- **Topical niches tied to a current event.** Demand evaporates; you're left with inventory of stale metadata.

## Differentiation angles inside a saturated niche

You can still win in a crowded niche if you pick one angle clearly:

- **Size angle.** "Extra Large Print," "Travel Size," "Jumbo Grid."
- **Audience angle.** "for Left-Handers," "for Truckers," "for Bird Watchers."
- **Theme angle.** "Classic Rock Lyrics Word Search," "Dog Breeds Word Search."
- **Format angle.** "Spiral Bound," "Tear-Out Pages" (note: KDP doesn't support spiral — use IngramSpark for that).
- **Difficulty angle.** "For Beginners," "Impossible Sudoku Only."

Pick exactly one. Don't stack four angles in the subtitle; it reads as spam.

## Signal to kill a niche fast

Run the niche scorer. If any of these hit, kill it:

- `saturation_flag: true` (competition sub-score < 40)
- Demand sub-score < 40
- Price sub-score < 40 (means the market prices <$6 or >$15 — bad unit economics either way)
- All top 10 listings have 1,000+ reviews (you cannot break in without 6+ months of paid ads)

## How many niches to test first

Start with **3 niches × 3 SKUs each = 9 SKUs**. That's your first quarter.
- 9 SKUs takes ~2 weeks of part-time work.
- You need 9 to see the hit-rate distribution.
- You must resist the urge to publish 9 SKUs in 1 niche — diversification across niches is the whole point.

After 90 days, scale into whichever niche produced a winner. Kill the other two or keep them as passive tail income.
